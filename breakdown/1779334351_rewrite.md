## Shooting Notes

- Lock off the tripod for all desk shots — the 2015/2026 contrast only works if both setups look nearly identical. The slight frame widening in Shot 6 is intentional and subliminal.
- Shot 3's pan should feel like the camera is *trying* to follow the creator, not a deliberate cinematic move — keep it loose and a half-beat late.
- Shot 4 push-in: slow digital zoom or lens pull, 1–2 seconds, barely perceptible. The effect should be felt, not seen.
- Shot 9 (B-roll): record your screen while JobWizard is actually autofilling a real form — the cursor moving through fields, text populating automatically, is the proof of concept. Film at a slight angle through the keyboard, not a flat direct screen capture. This shot is what converts skeptics.
- Background for all desk shots: bookshelf, printed resume, cold coffee. Avoid anything that reads "studio." Background objects silently signal "I'm a real person trying to get a job," not a sponsored creator.
- Sound: Chopin Nocturne No. 2 (moshimo sound design) or any solo piano with a bittersweet register. The music should feel slightly too pretty for how depleted the creator looks — that gap is part of the joke.
