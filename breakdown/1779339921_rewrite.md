## Hook

## Shooting Notes
- Single fixed camera throughout shots 1–8, self-shot at arm's length — replicate the static talking-head format exactly, no mid-shot adjustments
- Natural side or soft front window light only — avoid ring light flatness; slight lighting imperfection reads as authenticity on TikTok
- Gray or muted hoodie, no brand logos, minimal styling — "this is just my life right now" energy, not "I am filming a TikTok"
- Handheld transition at shot 9: physically pick up the phone, let the natural wobble happen, then set it down in front of the screen — recreates the visual gear-shift that resets viewer attention
- Screen recording (shots 9–10): high resolution but framed within the browser window, not full-screen — JobWizard panel clearly visible, no post-zoom needed; briefly hold on the match score and the cover letter generating
- Do NOT clean up the verbal "like, like, literally" pattern in shots 6–8 — the messiness is the credibility signal
- Eyes off-camera (up-left or side) during recall moments; eyes direct to lens during emotional peaks (shots 2 and 5 especially) — this alternation is the trust-building visual rhythm
- Audio: something with low-energy acoustic or lo-fi energy similar to "original sound — careerwithsarah" — intimate, not hype
